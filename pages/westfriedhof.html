What do you usually do commuting every day? I listen to music and scroll social networks or read something. Okay, then another question. Do you remember how, for example, in my case, Hackerbrücke station looks like? (Try to remember any of them you see every day). I failed.

My friend who uses the U1 line every day also failed. When I was telling him how our trip was and mentioned Westfriedhof station, he just asked me if there is something special. The station itself is special but very few people who are always busy and in a hurry can remember it.

The station built below Orpheusstraße was opened to traffic on 24 May 1998. It is served by the subway line U1. Since 12 December 2011, it has also been the terminus of the U7 reinforcement line, which only runs during rush hours.

I am a big fan of industrial architecture and I could not miss the chance to see this masterpiece. When you arrive at the platform, you are imposed upon by enormous domed lamps perched above your head. The lighting concept was designed by Ingo Maurer. There are 11 lamps, every 3.80 meters in diameter. They divide the space with swaths of blue, red, and yellow light.

By the way, my friend told me (when he finally stopped staring at his smartphone screen all the time) that the station was fascinating and worth visiting even if you don’t use the U1 line.

So, if you are bored with your view, stressed, or just need a new picture for your Instagram, go there, you won’t regret it. Someone wrote that these vibes remind New York. I have not been there, but “Challenge accepted!” for those who have.

And… Pay attention to the beauty that surrounds you. It is everywhere :)
