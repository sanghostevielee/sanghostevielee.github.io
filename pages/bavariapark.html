When you hear the word "Munich" what is the first that comes to your mind? For most of us, it will definitely be “bier”, “Oktoberfest”, “FC Bayern”, “Marienplatz”. Okay, what about “Oktoberfest”, “bier”, “fun”, “crowd”... If I show you this picture, are you able to connect it in any way with Oktoberfest?

Looks gloomy but hundreds of thousands of people come to Munich every year to visit this place, every year except 2020 :( Exactly, Theresienwiese is the official ground of the Munich Oktoberfest as well as Frühlingsfest, Tollwood-Winterfestival, and many more. Unfortunately, 2020 deprived us of Oktoberfest, and I don’t think that Frühlingsfest will take place. We have to live according to the new reality: now Theresienwiese serves as a place for a Corona test center.

Going further! We are here not to be sad. It was a cloudy winter day and we were too lazy to go somewhere far away. Bavaria Park was a perfect option because of its central location and… snail on the first picture from the google request. Liam told me that I had to go there because I had two snails as pets and needed one more.

If you miss museums during the lockdown Bavaria Park is the right place for you. Free museum under the sky where you can find a grove of figures and sculptures by artists from Munich.

Actually, we tried to find them all from the Wikipedia list but failed because we finally saw the snail statue. To be precise the snail statue belongs to the German Museum of Transportation. Anyway, it is a couple of steps from the park.

A couple of blocks from Bavaria Park you can find Endless Staircase – a beautiful and striking piece of public art. The staircase is in the outdoor courtyard of the KPMG building and looks like a double helix. At one time it looks like you could climb on it, but now it is impossible to check it because it is closed.

We finished our trips to the central station because we wanted “Pommes mit currywurst” and the closest place was there. So, Bavaria Park and places nearby show that even in the city center you can explore something new.
