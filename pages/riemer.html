Imagine: you are participating in the german version of Who Wants to Be a Millionaire? (Actually, I am not sure if it exists :)) and the last question: What BUGA is? So. one, two, three… Bundesgartenschau is a biennial federal horticulture show in Germany. I was shocked and then impressed. I have to admit that the Germans are really good at reconstructing old industrial areas and creating really nice green spaces with their own ideas and concepts.

Riemer Park is one more example of this transformation. But let’s back to BUGA. In 2005 it took place in Munich, on the site of the former airport. And exactly then and there the new park with its own see, nice pedestrian paths, and fancy playgrounds was presented. It was Riemer park, but you not to be confused sometimes it is called Buga park.

Riemer Park is an amazing place, especially in Summer. But we went there on the cold winter day and… it is still an amazing place. The park is extensive and characterized by a strictly geometric staging. On the walk, you will pass by the Riemer See, experience a beautiful view from the Rodelhill, and come to the last remaining part of the runway or the grandstand from 1939.

Riemer Lake looks amazingly natural in the constructed park. What you do not suspect: the lake is sealed at the bottom. Pumps ensure that groundwater flows into the basin and that a constant water level is guaranteed with good water quality.

Riemer Berg: Most of the time, the view extends over the trade fair city, the lake, and further into the Alps in the south and west. Incidentally, the mountain grew out of the demolition material from the former airport, the remains of the departure hall, or the arrival terminal. A look to the north reveals the afforestation of the Riemer wood. Unfortunately, I didn’t take a picture but isn’t it a good reason to go there and see everything in your own eyes. The view is breathtaking.

I won’t say what you can do in Riemer Park, this place is perfect for everything: for calm walking, for sport or for being a child for a while.

Except for the Seekiosk and the Riem Arcades, catering is, unfortunately, a bit poor. That’s why my rating is 4. Anyway, Riemer Park is absolutely a worth visiting place. I wish I could go there in Summer, but who knows.

— Dasha
