Olympiastadion Ghost-station

“

LIAM No ghosts, fake news 2/10
DASHA I don't need Ikea, I can find everything here
STEVIE This place reminds me of Fleetwood Mac.

”

This used to be a train station and now it’s not. What’s good about an abandoned station? That it’s abandoned! Apparently, the municipality doesn’t take care of this place and people just started to have fun here.

This whole place looks like a grey area. There are fences installed which indicates that you shouldn’t trespass, but people still enter here through loopholes that are easy to find. We also managed to climb over the fence and went down the stained staircase to the platform. There were already a lot of people downstairs – kids were posing for a cool photo and we even saw a family with small children, so we can assure you that this place is safe.

Still, the route we took was definitely not barrier-free and we actually found a better entrance from a nearby green space. We linked this spot in Google Maps below so that you don’t have to risk anything. Of course, you are also welcome to speak to your inner kid and climb over the fence.

Go down to the bottom where the railway tracks are and enjoy the crooked urban scenery of graffiti, a ruin of cement buildings and thrown away furniture. We picked up a wooden chair that somehow lost one of its legs and took a family photo with the instant camera Liam brought.

How did those random objects end up here? Maybe they shoot a hard rock music video where the singer destroys everything while the drummer is set on fire. Or maybe someone that was living beneath the station got a call from Studentenwerk that s/he can finally move into the dorm and left behind all the personal belongings. So we have no idea, but it seems like anything can happen here. And we all agreed that it would be mega fun to have a grill party here during the summer.

I grew up in Korea and Japan where every corner of a city has to be micro-managed and it is quite hard to imagine this kind of place could exist. And I’m starting to be convinced that people genuinely need to chill out because oftentimes, fun and joy unexpectedly sprout from the things that are as is.

— Stevie
