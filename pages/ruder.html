It was a normal Wednesday afternoon – I was in my room, tired from staring at my laptop from 8 am. And I chose to cook myself a huge lunch instead of pooping ideas for the mapping project, so all I could do was to smile awkwardly in our breakout session when Juliane came to see us during her friendly patrol.

“Actually, I have a place that I can recommend”, she said as if she noticed that I’ve had nothing much to say. She said that she was here with her friend and that they had a quality time.

Since then, Ruderregatta has been on the top of our list, nonetheless unvisited for almost two months. Why didn’t we check it out immediately? Well, this place is a little bit out of nowhere and the bus to there operates only on weekdays, so we had to wait for the perfect day to come – a warm and sunny Thursday afternoon.

This place was built as a venue of rowing and canoe for 1972 Summer Olympics. Almost 50 years old! You can tell its age from the racetrack signs that are faded, but everything is very well maintained. We sat on a deck and talked about how fun (or boring) it would be to watch people competitively rowing along the two-kilometer reservoir.

Although it was built as a racetrack, you will see that it is more like a cornered lake than an artificial swimming pool. It has small beaches with pebbles, grass, and a bench right by the waterfront. We sat on the bench and watched the sky turn pink like an iced tea. By the way, Liam said he could see the silhouette of the Alps from here. Do you think he saw it right?

— Stevie
