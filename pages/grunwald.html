It is difficult to sum up Grünwald in a short blog post due to the duality of its very existence.

On one hand Grünwald is the single wealthiest municipality in all of Germany, home to the rich and famous, whether they are famous conductors, known athletes, or film producers from the nearby Bavaria Film Studios, these ostentatious folks’ lifestyles grant us normies a strange & wonderful joy as we navigate their fancy neighbourhoods. We spent a decent chunk of our time in Grünwald gawking at minor celebrities excessively extravagant houses and personally I dreamed of the day that I too would be able to afford a Range Rover.

On the other hand, we also met a cool homeless guy who lives in a cave here, I believe he was homeless by choice as he gave up earthly possessions to chase enlightenment, regardless he seemed to enjoy the absolute vibes of nature by the Isar river in Grünwald, and how nobody bothered him except for us.

Our icon is a representation of Grunwald castle, which we chose as its beautiful structurally yet covered in gaudy, mildly ugly, colours, like a lot of things in the area.

I would recommend Grünwald as a visit for all, especially as it is a mere 20 minutes or so on a single Tram from Rosenheimer Platz, one of the hottest underground stations in Munich.
